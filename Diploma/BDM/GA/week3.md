### **Question 1 / 13**
**In B2B the end customer is**
*   **Correct Answer:** **B. Business**
*   **Explanation:** B2B stands for Business-to-Business. Unlike B2C (Business-to-Consumer) transactions where goods and services are sold to individual end-consumers for personal use, B2B transactions occur between two commercial entities. The purchaser of the product or service is another business, government agency, or institution that uses it for production, operations, or resale.

---

### **Question 2 / 13**
**When the products are purchased frequently despite the high prices, it is referred to as**
*   **Correct Answer:** **B. Straight rebuy**
*   **Explanation:** In organizational buying behavior, a "straight rebuy" is a routine purchasing situation where the business buyer reorders existing goods or services from an approved supplier without making any modifications. Because of established relationships, contract agreements, and the high transaction/switching costs associated with finding new vendors, organizations often make these frequent purchases automatically, even if the unit prices remain relatively high.

---

### **Question 3 / 13**
**Services tend to be rich in**
*   **Correct Answer:** **D. A and B only** (Experience and Credence attributes)
*   **Explanation:** Product attributes are categorized into search, experience, and credence qualities. 
    * *Search attributes* can be evaluated prior to purchase (common in tangible goods).
    * *Experience attributes* can only be evaluated during or after consumption (e.g., a vacation or a haircut).
    * *Credence attributes* are difficult for consumers to evaluate confidently even after consumption (e.g., medical diagnoses or complex legal services). 
    Due to their intangibility, services are predominantly high in experience and credence attributes.

### **Question 6 / 13**
**Consumers perceive higher risk when purchasing**
*   **Correct Answer:** **C. Services**
*   **Explanation:** Services are intangible, variable, and inseparable from their providers. Since consumers cannot physically inspect, test, or compare services before they buy them, they face greater uncertainty regarding the quality and outcome, leading to a higher level of perceived risk compared to purchasing physical goods.

---

### **Question 7 / 13**
**Impulse buying phenomenon occurs in**
*   **Correct Answer:** **A. B2C only**
*   **Explanation:** Impulse buying is an unplanned, emotionally driven decision. In B2B environments, purchasing processes are governed by formal budgets, professional procurement policies, technical specifications, and multiple decision-makers (the Buying Center). This structural rigor makes impulsive purchasing highly rare or practically non-existent in B2B.

---

### **Question 8 / 13**
**When purchases are made in B2B, all the 8 steps are followed in**
*   **Correct Answer:** **B. New task situation**
*   **Explanation:** The B2B buying process contains eight sequential steps (from problem recognition to performance review). In a "straight rebuy" or "modified rebuy," the organization skips or compresses several stages because they already have established suppliers and specifications. However, when facing a "new task" (buying a product/service for the first time), the buying center must carefully navigate every single one of the eight steps to minimize risk.

---

### **Question 9 / 13**
**B2B tends to be**
*   **Correct Answer:** **C. Price inelastic but conditional**
*   **Explanation:** In B2B markets, total industry demand is often price inelastic in the short term. This is because business buyers require specific components or materials to keep their manufacturing lines or operations running. A moderate price increase will not significantly decrease demand immediately because finding alternative inputs or changing production setups takes time.

---

### **Question 10 / 13**
**Agreements and contracts are higher in**
*   **Correct Answer:** **B. B2B due to higher purchase value**
*   **Explanation:** B2B transactions routinely involve large monetary volumes, customized products, strict delivery timelines, and long-term commitments. To mitigate risk, clarify legal liabilities, and lock in pricing, formal agreements and extensive contracts are much more common in B2B transactions than in lower-value, transactional B2C purchases.

---

### **Question 11 / 13**
**You work for ABC Software Technologies. The company decided to upgrade all its systems, and the purchase department has contacted four vendors to provide proposals based on their specifications. This proposal is referred to as**
*   **Correct Answer:** **B. RFQ** (Request for Quotation)
*   **Explanation:** A Request for Quotation (RFQ) is issued when an organization has already determined its exact technical specifications and is asking selected vendors to submit proposals outlining pricing and payment terms for those precise requirements. A Request for Information (RFI), on the other hand, is a more open-ended inquiry used earlier in the buying process to gather general market information.

### **Question 13 / 13**
**Of the four vendors identified by the purchase department, the internal department started rating for a vendor named "Turn AB", based on 4 CSF/KSFs- Pricing, Brand image, Service Quality and Market reputation. Given that ABC software technologies were relatively new, they were highly price sensitive. They allocated the next higher weightage to service quality. Allocate the weights as per this information. The weights decided were- 10%, 30%, 10% and 50%. They then gave a score of (1-5) where 1 was least and 5 was highest. The highest weightage was allocated a score of 5, the next highest a score of 4 and the remaining 2 KSFs were given a score of 1 each. What will be the final rating of "Turn AB"?**
*   **Correct Answer:** **B. 3.9**
*   **Step-by-Step Calculation:**

1. **Identify the Weights:**
   * The company is highly price-sensitive, so **Pricing** receives the highest weight: **50% (0.50)**.
   * **Service Quality** receives the next highest weight: **30% (0.30)**.
   * The remaining two KSFs (**Brand Image** and **Market Reputation**) each receive the remaining weights: **10% (0.10)** and **10% (0.10)**.

2. **Identify the Scores:**
   * The factor with the highest weight (Pricing) is assigned a score of **5**.
   * The factor with the next highest weight (Service Quality) is assigned a score of **4**.
   * The remaining two KSFs (Brand Image and Market Reputation) are assigned a score of **1** each.

3. **Calculate the Weighted Scores:**
   $$\text{Weighted Score} = (\text{Weight} \times \text{Score})$$
   * **Pricing:** $0.50 \times 5 = 2.5$
   * **Service Quality:** $0.30 \times 4 = 1.2$
   * **Brand Image:** $0.10 \times 1 = 0.1$
   * **Market Reputation:** $0.10 \times 1 = 0.1$

4. **Calculate the Final Rating:**
   $$\text{Total Rating} = 2.5 + 1.2 + 0.1 + 0.1 = 3.9$$
