Here are the solutions to all 15 questions along with their respective explanations.

---

### **Question 1 / 15**
* **Correct Answer:** **B. Willing and able to buy at different prices during a given period of time**
* **Explanation:** In economics, demand is defined as "effective demand." This means a consumer must not only desire a good or service (willingness) but must also have the purchasing power to pay for it (ability) at a specific price during a specific time frame.

---

### **Question 2 / 15**
* **Correct Answer:** **C. Quantity demanded decreases**
* **Explanation:** The Law of Demand states that, other factors remaining constant (*ceteris paribus*), there is an inverse relationship between the price of a good and its quantity demanded. Therefore, when price increases, the quantity demanded decreases.

---

### **Question 3 / 15**
* **Correct Answer:** **C. The quantity supplied also rises**
* **Explanation:** The Law of Supply states that, *ceteris paribus*, there is a direct (positive) relationship between price and quantity supplied. As the price of a good rises, producers are incentivized to produce and sell more of it to increase potential revenue.

---

### **Question 4 / 15**
* **Correct Answer:** **C. Downward sloping curve**
* **Explanation:** Because of the inverse relationship between price (typically plotted on the vertical y-axis) and quantity demanded (plotted on the horizontal x-axis), the demand curve slopes downward from left to right.

---

### **Question 5 / 15**
* **Correct Answer:** **B. The sum total of all individual demands for a product or service**
* **Explanation:** Market demand is derived by horizontally summing up the quantities demanded by every individual consumer in the market at each given price level.

---

### **Question 6 / 15**
* **Correct Answer:** **C. Quantity demanded equals quantity supplied**
* **Explanation:** Market equilibrium is the point where the market clears. This occurs at the price level where the quantity that buyers wish to purchase is exactly equal to the quantity that sellers wish to sell.

---

### **Question 7 / 15**
* **Correct Answer:** **B. The degree of responsiveness of demand to a change in price**
* **Explanation:** Price elasticity of demand measures how sensitive consumers are to a price change. It calculates the percentage change in quantity demanded divided by the percentage change in price.

---

### **Question 8 / 15**
* **Correct Answer:** **C. Elastic**
* **Explanation:** When the absolute value of the price elasticity of demand (PED) is greater than 1, it indicates that the percentage change in quantity demanded is larger than the percentage change in price, which is classified as elastic.

---

### **Question 9 / 15**
* **Correct Answer:** **B. Life-saving insulin for diabetic patients**
* **Explanation:** Inelastic demand occurs when consumers are relatively insensitive to price changes. Life-saving medical treatments, such as insulin, have very few or no close substitutes, making patients highly reliant on them regardless of price increases.

---

### **Question 10 / 15**
* **Correct Answer:** **B. How much quantity supplied changes in response to a change in price**
* **Explanation:** Price elasticity of supply measures the responsiveness of the quantity supplied of a good to a change in its price.

---

### **Question 11 / 15** *(Select all that apply)*
* **Correct Answer:** **B. Elastic demand, because the quantity demanded responded substantially to the price change**
* **Explanation:** If a price increase causes a high volume of subscribers to cancel, the quantity demanded is highly responsive to price. This is the definition of elastic demand. 

---

### **Question 12 / 15** *(Select all that apply)*
* **Correct Answer:** **B. Perfectly inelastic – demand remains constant regardless of price, so increasing price directly increases total revenue**
* **Explanation:** Theoretically, a life-saving drug with absolutely no alternatives represents a highly inelastic scenario. In simplified textbook economics, if a drug is completely indispensable for survival, demand is treated as perfectly inelastic (or near-perfectly inelastic) in the relevant price range, meaning consumers must buy it at any price, allowing the supplier to raise prices and directly increase total revenue. 
*(Note: Option D describes "inelastic but not perfectly" but incorrectly claims a small price increase leads to a "significant" fall in demand, which contradicts the definition of inelasticity).*

---

### **Question 13 / 15** *(Select all that apply)*
* **Correct Answer:** **B. Elastic demand: revenue falls; Inelastic demand: revenue increases**
* **Explanation:** 
  * If demand is **elastic**, the percentage decrease in quantity demanded is larger than the percentage increase in price, causing total revenue to fall.
  * If demand is **inelastic**, the percentage decrease in quantity demanded is smaller than the percentage increase in price, causing total revenue to rise.

---

### **Question 14 / 15** *(Select all that apply)*
* **Correct Answer:** **B. PED = 1.5 (elastic); Revenue falls from ₹1,00,000 to ₹84,000**
* **Explanation:** 
  * **PED calculation:** Price Elasticity of Demand = $\% \text{ change in quantity demanded} / \% \text{ change in price} = 30\% / 20\% = 1.5$. Since $1.5 > 1$, demand is elastic.
  * **Revenue calculation:** 
    * Initial Revenue: $1,000 \text{ units} \times \text{₹}100 = \text{₹}1,00,000$
    * New Revenue: $700 \text{ units} \times \text{₹}120 = \text{₹}84,000$
  * Because demand is elastic, the price increase results in a net drop in total revenue.

### **Question 15 / 15** *(Select all that apply)*
* **Correct Answers:** 
  * **A. Over time, non-OPEC producers expanded extraction capacity, increasing supply elasticity**
  * **B.** **Consumers gradually shifted toward fuel-efficient vehicles, increasing demand elasticity over time**
  * **C. In the long run, supply and demand curves become more elastic than in the short run**
* **Explanation:** 
  * **A is correct** because higher prices incentivized exploration and drilling in regions outside of OPEC (like the North Sea and Alaska), making long-run global supply more elastic.
  * **B is correct** because consumers cannot immediately change their consumption habits in the short run, but over the long run, they buy more fuel-efficient cars or find alternative heating sources, making demand more elastic.
  * **C is correct** because both buyers and sellers have more time to adapt to price signals over a longer horizon.
  * **D is incorrect** because the long-run adjustments in supply and demand eventually led to a collapse in oil prices in the 1980s.
